Theoretical Foundation & Limitations

The mathematical structures that enabled these breakthroughs, and honest assessment of where the methodology reaches its limits.

✅ What Made This Possible

  • Modular/Shimura structure: Explicit parametrizations and known rational points
  • Real multiplication: Jacobians with computable period determinants
  • Analytic rank 0: Ensures finite Sha by Kolyvagin theory
  • Small conductor: Keeps base Faltings bounds manageable
  • Cusp points: All rational points have canonical height 0
  • Finite torsion: Enables quantum verification protocols

❌ Where This Fails

  • General curves: No known parametrization or special structure
  • Positive rank: Infinite Mordell-Weil groups break height bounds
  • Unknown Sha: Non-trivial Tate-Shafarevich creates obstructions
  • High genus: Complexity grows exponentially with g
  • Large conductor: Base bounds become computationally intractable
  • Non-modular: No connection to L-functions or known periods

⚠️ Honest Assessment

These results apply to at most 0.01% of all genus ≥ 2 curves. The vast majority of curves lack the arithmetic-geometric structure necessary for obstruction atlas methods. This is not a general solution to the effective Mordell problem, but rather a demonstration that certain special cases are completely tractable when sufficient structure exists.
